Generate Product Development - Website

As a UX / UI Designer at Generate Product Development, I, along with a team of Designers of Developers, were tasked with completely redesigning the company website from the ground up. We worked the problem from research and personas right through to high fidelity wireframes and prototypes across a semester of intense work. We worked in Figma for the majority of this project before we handed it off to the developers. Here, our main focus was creating a website that felt more academically diverse than our current one, which very heavily focuses on the engineering side. Working closely with our clients and explaining our design choices along the way, our team rapidly extracted some complex branding and interface problems and proposed rigorous solutions to them within a very short timeframe.

SmartWealth

SmartWealth is a financial management application I developed as apart of my Interaction Design 2 course that is targeted at a graduating age group to make the transition to finacial independance easier. This began with service ideation, which was done through user research (interviews), followed by brand development, conceptual model and market analysis, heros journey and storyboarding, UI spec and VD Spec, and finally a UX experience prototype. This is one of the few times I have truly worked an idea from the absolute ground (no idea) via methods like creating a conceptual model and utilizing market analysis.

JIGSAW

JIGSAW is a portfolio building platform I built, targeting portfolio-oriented students at Northeastern looking to go on co-op. During my first co-op process I found very little time during the semester to build my own portfolio, and found websites like Wixx and Squarespace time consuming and overloading in terms of structure, information and animations. So, as a project during my Interaction Design class, I set out to solve this issue. This project started with building personas and potential user flows, right through user testing adn brand development, all the way to high fidelity wireframes. This was my first ever UX / UI project that was worked all the way through.

Canvas Re-design

For an initial project in my Interaction Design coursework, were tasked with redesigning an application that we thought was poorly designed from a workflow and UI standpoint. I chose canvas, a common choice by educational insititutions across the country to host coursework and educational content. This began with analysis of the poor parts of the current application including a breakdown of workflows and the base UI, followed by UI Spec and lo-fi reconstruction, VD Spec and hi-fi development.

ArtistLaunch

A project I completed during my time at Generate Product Development, ArtistLaunch aims to unify artists and playlist curators on applications like Spotify, Apple Music and Pandora. Through this relationship formed by ArtistLaunch, where curators browse and bid on artist's songs, negotiations can be made between two parties, enabling artists that might not otherwise have a wide reach or much visiblity to increase their exposure. I was the Design Lead on this project, where myself and 4 other designers worked this project from the ground up, right from workflows and mood-boards to a fully functional prototype in a hi-fi state.

App Development - Brigham Education Institute

During my time at Brigham and Woman's Hospital and Harvard Medical School, I spent a large portion of my time as the head UX and UI Designer on the BEI app development team. Our goal was to create an app for students, residents and medical staff that would store all educational material at the Brigham for easy access, a pain point identified by our project lead, Dr. Narath Carlile. As a designer, especially in a field such as healthcare, accessability was absolutely paramount to success and this is something I kept in mind throughout the project. In addition to UX and UI design, I also coded the front end of the email verification system we used using HTML, CSS and JS.
